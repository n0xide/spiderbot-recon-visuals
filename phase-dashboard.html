Single leg & control validation

Goal: prove the servo bus, IK, IMU, and PA612-CF print workflow on one leg before committing to 21 more servos.
2–3 weeks cumulative $400
Build deliverables
  • 1× leg printed in PA612-CF (coxa + femur + tibia + foot) with hollow cable channels (design.md §11.2)
  • 3× LX-224HV servos addressed (IDs 0x01–0x03) and bench-mounted
  • Teensy 4.1 with HAL-based bus driver firmware, BNO086 attached
  • Single-leg bench fixture clamping coxa to a fixed plate (testing.md §1)
  • 1× GX12 6-pin connector wired to verify modular-leg interface (design.md §11.1)
  • Walk-in-place stand built — printable or wood (testing.md §1). Do this early.

Cross-cutting practices

  • Every session ends with a log.md entry: date, attempted, worked, broke, next.
  • No new phase during another's debugging. If P1 is stuck, do P1 — don't escape into P2.
  • Calipers + scale every print. Track dimensional accuracy and mass; PA612-CF drifts with filament dryness.
  • Photo log. Phone photo at every assembly milestone → ../../hardware-documentation/spiderbot-recon-build-photos/
  • Concept gaps surface to ../study/ — gait math, EKF intuition, ROS 2 lifecycle, write a study note rather than guess-and-check.
