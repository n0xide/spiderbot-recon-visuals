Estop wiring detail

The hardware path

12V ──┬── 10 kΩ pull-up ───┬─── Buck #1 EN
       │                    │
       │                    └─── ┐
       │                         │
       │                  ┌──N.C.┘    (estop latched in:
       └── estop coil ────┘            contact OPEN ──→ EN floats)
                          │
                         GND

Estop pressed → N.C. opens → EN line floats (10 kΩ to 12 V isn't enough current to keep Buck #1 alive, designed-in) → Buck #1 shuts off → servos go limp. Compute (Buck #2/#3/#4) stays alive for graceful shutdown.

Why hardware, not software

Firmware bugs happen. A software-only estop can lock up. A hardware estop in series with the buck's EN pin cannot — physics, not code, kills the rail.

Test it every session (T1.8): press estop, verify rail dies within 50 ms on scope. If it doesn't latch, fix before powering servos.

Servo sub-bus star topology (schematic.md §3)

All 4 sub-buses tied at Teensy TX. Physically separated to keep wire runs short. Each tap has its own 22 µF + ferrite on power. Isolates single-bus faults.
