1 · 6S LiPo · the dominant fire risk

A 6S 8000 mAh pack contains 180 kJ of stored energy — ~40 g of TNT, released over seconds if abused. LiPos burn at 1500+ °C, emit toxic smoke, and cannot be extinguished with water.

▶
Charge in a LiPo-safe bag, on non-flammable surface (concrete/tile/metal). Never bed/carpet/wood.
▶
Never charge unattended. Set timer; check periodically.
▶
Charge at 1C max for first 5 cycles (1C = capacity in amps; 8 Ah → 8 A max). Faster after break-in but stick to 1C if not in a hurry.
▶
Storage voltage: 3.85 V/cell (23.1 V for 6S). Full-charge storage degrades + swells.
▶
Inspect before every use. Puffing, soft spots, scorch, solvent smell → retire.
▶
A crashed pack is suspect. Quarantine 24 h on non-flammable surface. Monitor temp.
▶
Class D extinguisher (lithium-rated) or metal bucket of dry sand. Water makes LiPo fires worse.
▶
Dispose: discharge to ~3.0 V/cell, tape leads, battery recycler. Never in regular trash.

2 · Servo motion · pinch and impact

24 servos × 30 kg·cm × 100 mm leg leverage = ~3 kg clamping force at the foot, more at intermediate joints. The robot can break a finger.

▶
Power-up: never have a hand inside the robot's reach envelope when applying battery power. The smart power-up sequence ([design.md §11.5]) helps — but a firmware bug can still cause unwanted motion.
▶
Estop is your friend. Test before every bench session.
▶
Bench-mount the robot. P0: clamp leg fixture. P1: low (10 cm) wooden block stand for initial gait. Lower to floor only when gait timing is debugged.
▶
Walk-in-place stand for gait debugging: feet in air, gait runs, robot stays put. Eliminates ~90% of impact risk during dev.
▶
Eye protection at first power-on. Servo gear failures occasionally throw plastic shards.
▶
Never reach between leg pairs while powered. Even "idle" — a network glitch can command sudden motion.

3 · Electrical · 24 V isn't lethal but burns

Mains is dangerous to life; 24 V from a LiPo is not. But 24 V at 50 A welds metal. A dropped tool across the battery terminals heats to >1000 °C and flash-burns whatever it touches.

▶
Insulate your tools. Tape screwdriver shanks. Use snub-nose pliers with insulated grips.
▶
One hand rule. When probing live circuits with a meter, keep one hand in your pocket — accidents don't go across your chest.
▶
Disconnect battery before any wiring change. Reconnect last.
▶
Tinned wire tips, no fraying. A stray strand bridging rails will trip protection or weld.
▶
Heat-shrink every solder joint on power wiring.
▶
FNIRSI DPS-150 first. Set 1 A limit for first power-on. If shorted/miswired, supply current-limits to safe value. Only when boot verified, connect battery.
▶
Cap exposed XT60 terminals when not connected.

Inrush current at first connection

Connecting charged battery to discharged cap bank = 100+ A momentary inrush. Spark at XT60, pitted contacts.

Cheapest fix: XT90-S in series (built-in pre-charge resistor). Chassis-side keeps XT60 for compatibility with other LiPos.

4 · ESD · the silent killer

Walking across carpet on a dry day → 5-15 kV static. Touching CMOS discharges this through the chip, can punch a hole in gate oxide. The chip might still work — or fail intermittently in a month, untraceably.

▶
Ground yourself before touching boards. Touch a known-grounded metal surface (plugged-in computer case). Better: ESD wrist strap clipped to ground.
▶
ESD mat if you can (~$30 with wrist strap).
▶
Avoid touching pin headers and IC legs. Hold boards by edges.
▶
Static-shielding bags for storage. Silver-foil bags > pink/black anti-static. Reuse what components came in.
▶
Humidity helps. <30% RH (typical winter heated indoor) = high ESD risk. Humidifier substantially reduces it.

ESD-sensitive on this build: Jetson Orin Nano, BNO086, RealSense, Lepton. Teensy and Pico are more forgiving but not immune.

5 · 3D printing · fumes, hot ends, sharp parts

Fumes

PA612-CF at 280-290 °C nozzle temp emits low concentrations of caprolactam, formaldehyde, other VOCs. Carbon fiber is bound in the polymer — won't aerosolize at print temp — but polymer breakdown products do.

▶
Print with enclosure closed (P1S / X1C / H2D have this; A1 / A1 Mini don't).
▶
Ventilate the print room. Open window, or vent printer to outdoors with 100 mm flex duct + small inline fan.
▶
HEPA / activated-carbon filter on enclosure if possible. X1C has one built in — check fresh.
▶
Don't sleep in the same room while printing. Print in workshop or garage if possible.
▶
N95 mask when sanding finished CF parts — carbon fiber dust is a respiratory hazard.

Hot ends

Bambu hot end runs > 280 °C for PA612-CF. Finger touch = serious burn, instant and deep.

▶
Wait for hot end <50 °C before touching. Printer displays temperature.

⚠ EMERGENCY · LiPo venting / smoking

  1. Get away. Do not breathe the smoke (HF and fluorinated compounds).
  2. If safe, move it outdoors or into a metal container.
  3. Sand or Class D extinguisher to smother. Not water.
  4. Do NOT pick up a smoking pack — even with gloves, the heat is severe.
  5. Ventilate the area afterward.

Physical deformation of any cell = retire immediately. A small dent shorts internal layers. Don't try to "use it up."

Phase-specific safety adds

P0 · single leg

  • Bench supply, current limit 1 A initial → 5 A after smoke test
  • Single-leg fixture clamped to bench
  • No battery yet — DPS-150 only

P1 · walk (first LiPo)

  • Read §1 LiPo rules again. First time handling 6S.
  • Charge LiPo from 0 to 100% under direct observation 3 times before unattended
  • Cap bank inrush — XT90-S anti-spark mandatory
  • Estop reachable from sitting position

P2 · see

  • ESD precautions when handling Jetson + RealSense + BNO086
  • Verify Buck #2 EN tied through Teensy — Jetson powers up only after safe-pose

P3 · map

  • Livox eye-safe Class 1 laser — but don't stare directly into the aperture
  • Vibration isolation on LiDAR mount — sensor failure if vibrating excessively

P4 · recon (field)

  • Conformal coat — spray outdoors / well-ventilated
  • LoRa: never transmit without antenna attached (RF energy back into PA destroys it)
  • 915 MHz: legal in US, check local regs if traveling

P5 · polish

  • WS2812B strips can pull > 3 A when full white — verify rail headroom
  • Vinyl wrap: ventilate during heat-set
Use at the start of each session.
