Why more legs = more stable (concepts.md §1)

A walking robot stays upright when its center of mass projects onto the ground inside the convex hull of its planted feet — the support polygon. Larger polygon, more inside-margin, less likely to tip.

  • Quadruped, 1 leg up: 3-foot triangle. Marginal — small CoM shifts tip the robot.
  • Hexapod, tripod gait, 3 legs up: 3-foot triangle. Same marginal state most of the cycle.
  • Octopod, wave gait, 1 leg up: 7-foot heptagon. Very stable.
  • Octopod, tetrapod, 4 legs up: 4-foot quad. Comparable to hexapod tripod but with more foot placement options.
