Why calibration matters Servos installed without offsets walk subtly tilted. Uncalibrated IMU gives bad pose. Uncalibrated FSRs miss contact events. Doing it badly produces robots that don't work right and you can't figure out why.

Pre-session checklist (calibration.md §13)

P0/P1 P0/P1

1 · Servo zero-position offsets (24 servos)

Each servo's mounted spline orientation is a few degrees off ideal. Calibration measures the offset and stores it. Without offsets, IK commands and physical positions disagree by 2-5°, walking is subtly tilted.

Procedure

1
Power up leg on bench fixture (or robot on walk-in-place stand). Commanded angles = factory zero.
2
For each coxa: mechanically position the leg so the femur points straight out from the body radially. Read servo position via bus protocol. offset_coxa = reading − expected_zero.
3
For each femur: hold the femur horizontal. Read position. offset_femur = reading − 0°.
4
For each tibia: hold tibia vertical (knee fully extended, foot pointing straight down). Read. offset_tibia = reading − 90°.
5
In firmware: command = ik_result + offset[joint] before sending to bus.

Mirror-direction (right side +1, left side −1)

Right-side servos rotate clockwise on positive command; left-side mirror counter-clockwise. IK math expects one convention. Apply: final = (ik + offset) × direction.

3 · Servo joint-limit calibration

Each leg has a reachable range narrower than the servo's full span. Hitting a mechanical hard-stop under load breaks gear teeth.

Procedure

1
Leg de-energized. You move each joint to its mechanical limit manually.
2
Read position via bus (encoder reports even when un-powered).
3
Record min/max per joint. Set software limits 5° inside mechanical limits for margin.

4 · IMU calibration (BNO086)

Accelerometer + gyroscope (auto)

Magnetometer (manual figure-8)

1
Power up BNO086 in calibration mode.
2
Pick up the robot, hold in your hands.
3
Move through a figure-8 pattern, rolling all axes, for ~30 s.
4
Verify status reaches "fully calibrated" via BNO086 SH-2 self-status register.

⚠ Magnetometer unreliable near 24 servos. Mount IMU as far from them as possible (top of carapace). If results bad, disable mag in fusion mode (yaw drifts but works in motor-noisy env).

8 · Foot FSR calibration

Each FSR402 has different resistance under the same applied force. Baseline + scale per foot needed for consistent contact detection.

Procedure

1
Robot powered up, legs in air on walk-in-place stand. Record each FSR's unloaded raw ADC value (the baseline).
2
Press each foot with 1 kg load on a scale. Record loaded ADC value.
3
Per-foot scale: force_N = (raw_adc − baseline) × scale_factor where scale_factor makes 1 kg = ~10 N.
4
Threshold: 30% of calibrated 1 kg value (~3 N contact). Below = "in air", above = "in contact".

9 · INA226 calibration

INA226 has a CALIBRATION register set by shunt resistor value + expected current range. For 0.002 Ω shunt and 30 A range:

Current_LSB = 30 A / 32768 = 0.000915 A/bit
CAL = 0.00512 / (Current_LSB × 0.002) = 2796

Write 2796 to register 0x05 (CALIBRATION).

6 · Livox MID-360

Factory calibrated. Verify on first use, then measure mechanical transform from LiDAR mount to base_link with calipers + inclinometer (5 mm / 1° accuracy is enough to start).

11 · robot_localization EKF tuning

EKF fuses IMU + foot odometry + LiDAR odometry → /odom. Many tunable covariances. Default settings rarely work well. Plan a session, expect 2-4 hours.

Quick-tune procedure

1
Walk robot a known distance (e.g., 5 m forward).
2
Compare EKF reported distance to ground truth.
3
Over-estimates → increase IMU covariance OR decrease foot-odom covariance. Under-estimates → opposite. Iterate.
P3 advanced P3

12 · Camera↔LiDAR extrinsic

Mechanical measurement gives ~1-2 cm error. For tight projection: use a lidar_camera_calibration ROS 2 package, point both at a checkerboard, solve.

Skip this if SLAM works fine without it. Most indoor recon tolerates 1-2 cm misalignment.

10 · Mic array timing

DoA measures sub-millisecond timing differences. Mics must have known geometry + low audio path latency variance.
