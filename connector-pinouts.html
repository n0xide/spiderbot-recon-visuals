Servos daisy-chain — each servo has IN + OUT 3-pin sockets. The OUT plugs into the next servo's IN.

Sub-bus star wiring

From the chassis hub, one cable runs to the first servo of each sub-bus. From there it daisy-chains through the leg's 3 servos, jumps to the second leg's coxa via the GX12 connector spare pin (or a short jumper at the GX12), and chains through that leg.

Hub ──┬── 22µF + ferrite ──┬── L1 coxa → L1 femur → L1 tibia
      │                    └── L2 coxa → L2 femur → L2 tibia    (sub-bus A)
      │
      ├── 22µF + ferrite ──┬── L3 → L4    (sub-bus B)
      ├── 22µF + ferrite ──┬── L5 → L6    (sub-bus C)
      └── 22µF + ferrite ──┬── L7 → L8    (sub-bus D)

       │           │

Voltage divider: unpressed ≈ 0.3 V (low), pressed ≈ 3.0 V (high). 12-bit ADC at 1 kHz. Contact threshold ~1.5 V in firmware.

FSR sits between the TPU heel pad and the tibia tip. Wires run inside the tibia → coxa → GX12 pin 4.

Crimping & wiring DOs and DON'Ts

✓ DO

  • Tin wire ends with solder if going into screw terminals (not for crimps — solder makes crimps brittle)
  • Heat-shrink every solder joint on power wiring
  • Use the Klein 3000CRKIT or SOMELINE ratchet crimper — Klein for power, SOMELINE for 22-32 AWG signal
  • Pull-test every crimp at 2× expected mechanical stress
  • Label both ends of each cable with a Brother PT label (or vinyl)
  • Run +V and GND as a twisted pair where currents are dynamic (servo bus)
  • Star-ground at the chassis hub: one common ground point, single 12 AWG drop to battery negative

✗ DON'T

  • Solder a crimp barrel — solder wicks up the wire and creates a stress concentration that fatigue-fails
  • Splice power wires with twist + tape — always crimp or solder-and-heatshrink
  • Run signal cables under servos — magnetic interference corrupts data lines
  • Re-use crimped pins from old harnesses — the deformed brass cracks on the second crimp
  • Use solid-core wire for anything moving (legs!) — solid core fatigue-fails. Stranded silicone for everything
  • Use uninsulated banana plugs on a 12 V bus — a slip and you weld your meter probe
  • Forget to disconnect the battery before any wiring change
